      • Why LLP?
        • provides limited liability but allows flexibility of organizing internal structure
        • enables entrepreneurs, professionals and enterprises to form commercially efficient vehicles suited to their requirements.
        • suitable for small enterprises and for investment by venture capital
      • Meaning of LLP
        • alternative corporate business vehicle
        • gives benefits of limited liability at low compliance cost
        • flexibility of organising internal structure
        • seperate legal entity, LLP liable for full extent of its assets, liability of its partners is limited
        • called Hybrid b/w a company and a partnership

      • Characteristic / Features of LLP
        • Body Corporate
          • legal entity seperate from partners, perpetual succession
          • Any change in partners shall not affect anything
        • Perpetual succession
          • capable of holding property and entering into contracts in its own name
        • Seperate legal entity
          • liable to the full extent of its assets
          • liability of partners limited
          • creditors of LLP shall be creditors of LLP alone
        • Mutual Agency
          • No partner liable on account of independent or un-authorized actions of other partners
          • No partner can bind the other partner through their acts
          • Partners are agents of the LLP and not each other
        • LLP agreement
          • Partners can make up any agreement
          • In absence of such agreements, LLP to be governed by provisions of the Act.
        • Artificial Legal Person
          • created by law and clothed with rights of an individual.
          • Can do everything except take oath, be sent to jail, do a learned profession.
          • invisible, intangible, immortal but not fictitious cus it exists.
        • Common Seal
          • Not mandatory
          • Shall remain in custody of official and be affixed in presence of two designed partners
        • Business for profit only
        • E-Filling of documents
          • in computer readable form, authenticated by a partner by the use of electronic signature.
        • Foreign LLP
          • a limited liability partnership formed, established and registered outside India but place of business in India.

      • Incorporation
        • Incorporation Document (Section 11)
          • Incorporation Document
            • Two or more persons subscribe their name to incorp doc.
            • in form and with fees as may be prescribed
            • Name of LLP
            • Business of LLP
            • Address of reg. office of LLP
            • Name and address of each partners
            • Name and address of designed partners
          • Statement
            • Along with incorp doc
            • made by either Advocate, CS, CA , Cost accountant
            • that all requirements of this Act and the rules made thereunder have been compiled with,
            • in respect of incorporation and matters precedent and incidental thereto.
            • If person knows this info to be false shall be punishable
              • term of upto 2 years
              • fees 10000 - 5 laks
        • Incorporation by registration (Section 12)
          • When all is done, Registrar may keep the incorporation document
          • within 14 days, register the incorp document and give a certifcate that LLP is incorporated
          • Statement may be sufficient evidence that all requirements are compiled with
        • Registered Office of LLP and its change (Section 13)
          • RG may be used to send all communication by post or any other address specifically declared by the LLP in manner prescribed
          • May change RG with notice, shall take effect only upon such filing
          • If any provisions contravened then punishable by 2000-25000
        • Effect of registration
          • doing things which body corporates do
          • suing and being sued
          • holding/disposing any kind of property
          • having a common seal if decide to
        • Name (Section 15)
          • shall have "LLP" or its full form at the end of their name
          • name shall not be
            • undesirable / resembling other trade mark or name
        • Reservation of name (Section 16)
          • person may apply in such form and manner as prescribed and accompanied by fees to the Registrar for the reservation of a name set out in the application as
          • Name will be reserved for 3 months
        • Change of name (Section 17)
          • if problems with name
          • CG may direct the LLP to change its name within 3months or such longer period
            • punishable by 10000 - 5 lakhs fine if not compiled with
            • designated partner by 10000 to 1 lakh
        • Steps of registration
          • Reservation of name using e-form 1
          • e-form 2 for incorporating a new LLP and consent of partners is also a doc
          • Execution of LLP agreement, LLP agreement to filed with registrat within 30 days of incorporation
      • Partners and their relations
        • Relationship of partners (Section 23)
          • Mutual rights governed by agreement
          • In absence of agreement, provisions as set-out in the First Schedule will govern
          • Agreement in writing b/w partners before LLP incorporation may impose obligations on the LLP and should be ratified by all partners after incorporation of LLP
        • Cessation of partnership interest (Section 24)
          • may cease to be partner in agreement with other partners
          • or with 30 days notice
          • will be automatically removed if
            • his death, dissolution of LLP
            • unsound mind
            • applied to adjudged as an insolvent or declared as an insolvent
          • If someone has ceased, he'll still be called a partner unless
            • the person with whom LLP is dealing with has notice that former partner has ceased to be a partner
            • notice to the registrat
          • Former partner/ a person entitled to his share will be entitled to
            • amount equal to capital contribution
            • share of accumulated profits as at the date of his cessation
            • No right to interfere in management of LLP
        • Registration of changes in partners (Section 25)
          • Change in name of partner or cessation
            • Partner shall inform LLP within 15 days of change in name and address
              • LLP to file notice with registrar within 30 days
            • In partner cessation, file notice with Registrar within 30 days
            • A partner may file the notice himself if he believes LLP won't do it.
              • Such notice received by Registrar will be confirmed from LLP and if no reply received from LLP within 15 days, then registrar will fine this notice
          • New partner coming
            • Consent of him becoming partner, duly authenticated in the manner as may be prescribed.
            • All such notices signed by the designed partner and authenticated in the manner as may be prescribed
          • If LLP doesn't give notices, the LLP and every designed partner - fine of 2000 - 25000
          • if partner doesn't give notice to LLP about his name change then fine of 2000-25000
      • Extent and Limitation of liability of LLP and Partner
        • Partner as agent (Section 26)
          • Agent of LLP not other partners
        • Extent of liability
          • Not bound if
            • Partner has no authority of act on behalf of LLP in a particular act
            • and the person he is dealing with has full knowledge of this
          • Liable to person to whom partner is liable due to his wrongful doing in normal course of business
          • Obligation of LLP whether arising in contract or otherwise shall be obligation of LLP only
          • Liabilites to be met out of property of LLP
        • Extent of liability of partners
          • Not personally liable solely by reason of being a patner
          • will be liable for his own wrongful act or omission
            • but for his own act only, not other partners
        • Holding out (Section 29)
          • Any person who represents himself to be a partner and the other party believes him and on such believe the party gives credit to LLP, the holding out guy is liable to such person
          • However, LLP shall be liable to the extent of credit received by it, without prejudice to the liability of person so holding out
          • If a person's name is being used after his death then his estate will not be liable.
        • Unlimited liability in case of fraud Section 30
          • If doing act with intent to defraud creditors or anyone
            • Unlimited liability of LLP and its partners who were party to such fraud
          • If done without knowledge or the authority of LLP then LLP is not liable
          • Any party to such fraudlent business shall be punishable with
            • term upto 2 years
            • 50000-5 lakhs rs
          • Without any prejudice to such criminal proceedings, LLP / Designated partner / Employee shall be liable to pay compensation to any person who suffered any loss or damage but LLP not liable if Partner/Employee acted fraudulently without knowledge of LLP
        • Whistle Blowing(Section 31)
          • The Court or Tribunal may reduce, waive any penalty leviable against any person who may provide useful info during investigation or when any information given by them is used to convict LLP/Partner/Employee
          • No Employee/Partner may be discharged, demoted, suspended, threatened, harassed or in any other manner discriminated solely because of his providing informationt o LLP
      • Financial Disclosures
        • Maintenance of books of account, other records and audit (Section 34)
          • Proper books of accounts
            • maintain such books ambp , relating to its affairs for each year of its existence
            • on cash basis or accural basis
            • using double entry system and shall maintain the same at its registered office
            • for such period ambp
          • Statement of Account and Solvency
            • Within 6 months of end of each FY
            • Prepare this as at end of such FY
            • signed by designed partners of LLP
          • File the SAS with registrar within prescribed time every year in form/manner/fees ambp
          • Accounts of LLP be audited in accordance with rules. CG may by notification exempt any LLP or classes of LLP from such requirement
          • LLP may be punishable who fails to comply with 25000 - 5 lakhs
          • Designated partner may be punishable by 10000 - 1 lakh
        • Annual return (Section 35
          • LLP shall file annual return duly authenticated with the registrar within 60 days of closure of FY
          • if fails then fineable by 25000 - 5 lakhs
          • designated partner fineable by 10000- 1 lakhs
      • Conversion into LLP
        • Registration
          • Registrar on being satisfied that that to-be-LLP has complied with provisions and register the documents, issue a certificate of registration stating that LLP exists from date specified
          • LLP shall within 15 days inform Registrar of Companies or of Firms
          • The now-partner former-shareholders will be bound by the provisions of act upon such conversion
          • Effects of conversion will be applicable from such date
        • Effect of registration
          • There shall be a LLP by the name specified in certificate
          • All property, obligations and whole of the undertaking of the firm or company as the case may be shall be transferred to and shall vest in the LLP without any kind of assurance, act or deed and
          • The firm or company shall be deemed to be dissolved and removed from records of Registrar
      • Foreign LLP (Section 59)
        • CG may make rules in relation to establishment of place of business by foregin LLP within India and carrying on business by applying or incorporating with such modifications as appear appropriate
      • Winding Up and Dissolution
        • May be either voluntary or by Tribunal
        • Circumstances in which LLP may be wound up Tribunal (Section 64)
          • if LLP decides that LLP be wound up by the Tribunal
          • If no of partners are below 2 for more than 6 months
          • LLP unable to pay debts
          • LLB acted against interests of sovereignty and integrity of India, the security of the State or public order
          • LLP made default in filing SAS or annual return for any 5 consecutive years
          • or if Tribunal is of the opinion that it is just that SAS be wound up
      • Miscellaneous
        • Business transaction of LLP and Partner
          • Partner may lend money to and transact other business with the LLP and has the same rights as any other debtor
        • Application of Provisions of Companies Act
          • CG may by notification in official gazette specify the provisions which shall apply to LLP in any manner
          • Such notification be laid before each house for a total period of 30 days for any amount of sessions and if both houses agree that it shall be issued, only then can it be issued.
        • Electronic filing of the documents
          • Any copy of document which is signed by Registrar and certified is to be admissible as of equal validity with the original doc
          • If a extract from a doc with registrar is presented as evidence then it shall be considered as such
        • Payment of Additional fees
          • Any delayed document may be filed within 300 days of such delay with 100 rs fine per of such delay
